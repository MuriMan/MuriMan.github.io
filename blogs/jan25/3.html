Transforming Dhaka: A Smart City Simulation

Introduction (30 seconds)

  • Opening Statement:
    "Imagine a Dhaka where traffic flows seamlessly, waste is managed efficiently, and resources are utilized sustainably. Through technology, this vision can become reality. Welcome to our solution for Urban Overload: Using a simulated Dhaka as a testing ground for transformative urban changes."

  • Problem Overview:
    Dhaka, like many rapidly growing cities, faces challenges such as traffic congestion, inefficient infrastructure, and resource scarcity. Without affordable access to supercomputers, accurate planning is limited.

Core Idea (1 minute)

Solution: Use a modded version of Cities: Skylines 2 to simulate Dhaka‚Äôs urban dynamics, enabling cost-effective, detailed analysis of proposed changes.

  • Why Cities: Skylines 2?

    • Customization: The game allows detailed city-building with adjustable parameters for traffic, zoning, utilities, and public services.
    • Affordability: Requires only a capable PC, not high-cost infrastructure.
    • Community Support: Offers a rich ecosystem of mods and tools for highly accurate simulations.

  • Steps to Implement:

    1. Model Creation: Build a scaled version of Dhaka in Cities: Skylines 2 using open-source geospatial data, like OSM (OpenStreetMap), to replicate road networks, landmarks, and population density.
    2. Problem Identification: Use the simulation to highlight current issues such as congestion hotspots, waste management inefficiencies, or insufficient green spaces.
    3. Tech Solutions Testing: Experiment with various interventions, including:
      • Smart traffic lights synchronized via AI algorithms.
      • Dedicated lanes for public transport.
      • Waste-to-energy plants for sustainable waste management.
      • Smart sensors for water and energy usage.
    4. Cost-Benefit Analysis: Assess each intervention's impact and financial feasibility through in-game data metrics and performance dashboards.

Social Impact (45 seconds)

  3. Livability: Enhanced urban planning promotes healthier, more sustainable lifestyles.
  4. Scalability: A Dhaka-specific simulation can inspire other cities in developing countries to adopt affordable planning tools.
