Mathematics and the populus

January 2025.

Humanity's greatest achievement is mathematics. It is also the most hated of humanity's creations, whilst being, to some people, very loved. I would like to speak my mind about the duality of maths through this weblog.

Mathematics emerged as ways to quantify quantities. Starting as a way to keep track of grain and finance, to quantifying the workings of the universe. That is all mathematics is, representations of amounts and relationships between multiple of these amounts.

Since childhood, the majority of the schooled population of earth are taught to blindly memorise instructions on how to do maths. Countless methods have emerged to teach children counting, addition, subtraction, multiplication and division, some more frustrating than others. In fact, for a large part of humanity, this is where the hatred against mathematics sprouts. For the basic operations mentioned, often these are the only ways to teach not only children, but rather, anybody.

The problem arises when fractions are discussed. Around this time, maths is completely disassociated from the reality of its concepts, that of the dealing of amounts. Students are given instructions on how fractions can be added, subtracted, divided and multiplied, and are often left with no clue as to why the instructions are what they are. Students are taught to blindly practice pages upon pages of such exercises, of which they will get a lot wrong, for which they will be penalised, creating spite and resentment against an inanimate set of thoughts.

I have personally went down this route. Famillial pressures forced into me anxiety regarding maths, which anxiety followed me into exam halls and the plethora of sub-par results. I came to the multiple epiphanies once school started after lockdown. Certain social situations led me to end up spending hours with no distractions. In this time, I stayed in my head thinking about what school taught me. It started with the concepts of algebra, and the physical implications of algebraic operations. Slowly, mathematics revealed to me, its beauty.

I believe, the majority of school systems should adopt methods that uphold understanding over memorisation. This includes testing systems that reward the former over the latter, opposite to what is currently effective. Such a system should, in fact, develop generally intelligent adults, that occasionally intelligent individuals.
